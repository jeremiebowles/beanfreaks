**Theme:** Vitamin D in summer
**Type:** Seasonal angle
**Image:** `vitamin-d3-0.webp`
**Page:** beanfreaks.com/viridian/vitamin-d3/

**GBP / Facebook:**
> Most people stop taking vitamin D in summer. It's worth thinking twice about that.
>
> The UK sun is strong enough to trigger vitamin D production between roughly April and September — but only between 11am and 3pm, only when enough skin is exposed, and only on the days it actually appears. If you're office-based, or you're wearing SPF (which blocks synthesis), your summer levels may not be as high as you'd expect.
>
> We stock Viridian's vegan D3 in three strengths — 400iu, 1000iu and 2000iu — all derived from lichen rather than lanolin, making them genuinely suitable for vegans. If you're not sure which strength makes sense for you, come in and ask.

### Week 3 — 30 June 2026
**Theme:** Not all magnesium is the same
**Type:** The quiet thing
**Image:** `magnesium-bisglycinate-0.webp`
**Page:** beanfreaks.com/viridian/magnesium-bisglycinate/

**GBP / Facebook:**
> Not all magnesium supplements are the same — and it's worth knowing the difference before you buy.
>
> Magnesium oxide is the cheapest and most common form you'll find in pharmacies. It has poor absorption and is the form most likely to cause digestive discomfort. Magnesium citrate absorbs considerably better and is a solid everyday option. Magnesium bisglycinate absorbs differently again — the body recognises it as an amino acid rather than a mineral, which means less competition for absorption and considerably less gut irritation.
>
> If you've tried magnesium before and found it didn't suit you, it was almost certainly the form rather than magnesium itself. We stock all three at different price points — come in and we'll point you in the right direction.

### Week 7 — 28 July 2026
**Theme:** How to read a supplement label
**Type:** The quiet thing
**Image:** any Viridian product with visible label detail — `high-twelve-b-complex-1.webp` works well

**GBP / Facebook:**
> A few things worth looking for on a supplement label before you buy:
>
> **Form matters.** Magnesium citrate absorbs better than oxide. Methylcobalamin B12 is the active form — cyanocobalamin needs converting. The cheaper versions of most minerals and vitamins use forms your body works harder to use.
>
> **The "other ingredients" section.** This is where the fillers live — magnesium stearate, silicon dioxide, microcrystalline cellulose. They're not necessarily harmful, but they mean the capsule isn't 100% active ingredient. Viridian and Terra Nova, which we stock, publish no-filler formulas.
>
> **Dose per serving vs dose per capsule.** Some labels quote a dose per two or three capsules. Worth checking you're comparing like for like.
>
> We're happy to help you compare products in store — bring in what you're currently taking if you want a second opinion.
