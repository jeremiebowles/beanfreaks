## What is Biona?

Biona is an organic wholefood brand that has been running since the early 2000s, distributed across Europe and widely available in independent health food shops in the UK. The range covers everyday pantry staples — oils, tinned goods, pasta, cereals, condiments — all made to organic certification standards.

The appeal is straightforward: Biona products are certified organic, reliably available, and competitively priced for the quality. They are not a specialist supplement brand. They are what you reach for when you want the everyday staples in your kitchen to be genuinely organic rather than just labelled as natural.

## The range

### Oils

Biona's coconut oil is one of their best-known products. They produce both raw virgin coconut oil (cold-pressed, unrefined, with the natural coconut flavour intact) and a refined version for higher-heat cooking where you do not want the coconut taste. Both are organic.

Their extra virgin olive oil is cold-pressed and certified organic. If you use olive oil daily — for dressing, cooking, or both — switching to Biona is one of the simpler upgrades you can make to your pantry without changing anything about how you cook.

### Tinned goods

Biona's organic tinned range covers the staples: chickpeas, cannellini beans, kidney beans, black beans, lentils, and chopped tomatoes. The tins use BPA-free lining.

For anyone cooking plant-based meals regularly, an organic tinned range matters. Conventionally grown pulses and tomatoes are frequently produced with pesticide residues that remain in the product. Choosing organic here is one of the higher-impact switches you can make given how often these ingredients appear.

### Coconut products

Beyond coconut oil, Biona produce coconut water, coconut cream, and coconut milk. Their organic coconut bars — dark chocolate-coated coconut pieces — are a popular grab-and-go option and regularly sell out at all three of our stores.

### Pasta and cereals

Biona produce a range of organic pasta in various formats. Their cereal and muesli range is worth knowing about if you are looking for organic breakfast options beyond what the main supermarkets carry.

### Condiments

Their organic apple cider vinegar with mother, tamari, and soy sauce are consistent sellers. If you are using ACV regularly for its purported benefits, the with-mother version is the one to choose — it retains the active culture that gives raw ACV its properties. The Biona version is unpasteurised and unfiltered.

## Why organic for pantry staples?

There is a reasonable argument that for some products — particularly thick-skinned fruits you peel — the organic premium is not worth paying. Pantry staples are a different case.

Oils concentrate whatever was used in growing the crop. Tinned tomatoes and pulses are consumed in significant quantities over time. Cereals are eaten daily. Choosing organic for the things you use most frequently and in largest quantities is a sensible place to focus if you cannot switch everything.

Biona's positioning — broad range, consistent quality, organic certification, available in independent shops rather than only the premium supermarkets — makes them a practical choice rather than an aspirational one.
