Peanut butter is one of those things that sounds simple but varies enormously depending on what is actually in the jar. Most of what fills supermarket shelves is a processed product with added sugar, palm oil, and salt mixed into the peanuts. Natural peanut butter is a different thing entirely.

Here is what separates them and what we stock at Beanfreaks.

## What is natural peanut butter?

Natural peanut butter contains one ingredient: peanuts. Sometimes a small amount of salt. Nothing else.

Regular supermarket peanut butter typically contains peanuts plus added sugar, partially hydrogenated or fully hydrogenated vegetable oil (often palm), salt, and sometimes emulsifiers. The oils and emulsifiers are added to prevent separation and give the product a smooth, uniform texture that stays stable on the shelf.

Natural peanut butter separates. The peanut oil rises to the top because there is nothing holding it in suspension. This is not a quality defect: it is what peanut butter does without additives. Stir it before each use and it is fine.

## Why it matters

**Palm oil:** Conventional peanut butter brands rely heavily on palm oil to stabilise the texture. Palm oil production is one of the leading drivers of deforestation in Southeast Asia and is strongly linked to habitat loss for orangutans, tigers, and other species. Avoiding it in everyday products like peanut butter is one of the easier swaps to make.

**Sugar:** Added sugar in peanut butter is unnecessary. Peanuts have a naturally rich, slightly sweet flavour on their own. A tablespoon of regular peanut butter can contain as much as 3g of added sugar.

**Hydrogenated fats:** Partially hydrogenated oils contain trans fats, which are associated with cardiovascular risk. Fully hydrogenated oils are better than partially hydrogenated but are still an additive that natural peanut butter simply does not need.

## How to use natural peanut butter

The main adjustment is stirring. Natural peanut butter separates on standing, so give it a good stir when you open a new jar and again each time you use it. Keeping the jar upside down when not in use helps minimise separation.

Beyond toast, natural peanut butter works well in:

- Porridge, stirred in for extra protein and fat
- Smoothies, blended with banana, oat milk, and a little cacao
- Satay sauce, thinned with soy sauce, lime juice, garlic, and a little warm water
- Baking, in cookies or energy balls
